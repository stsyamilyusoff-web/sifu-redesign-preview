A vs B - the founder decision

DimensionA · Brand-mark splashB · Photo splash
FeelClean, fast, instantly recognisable. Reads as "an app booting" the way most apps do.Warm, human, emotional. Says "real tutors, real students" from frame one.
Brand readingBrand IS the screen - the mark + colour are the whole canvas; zero ambiguity.Brand reads through a solid chip over the imagery; strong, but the photo is the lead.
Fit for a 300ms bootIdeal - a boot frame is on screen for a blink; a calm mark is all the eye needs.A rich photo for ~300ms can feel like a flash; better suited to a screen you dwell on.
PerformanceInstant - a gradient + an SVG, nothing to fetch or decode.A full-bleed photo must decode at launch; on a cold start that risks a beat of blank.
Photography directionDoesn't use it (the direction allows the mark as the one exception for the boot frame).Applies it - but the direction's emotional payoff lands harder on the carousel (which already uses photography) than on a sub-second boot.
RiskLow. The only "risk" is it being unremarkable - which is correct for a boot screen.A stock-feeling photo on the FIRST frame sets a generic tone (the Headspace cautionary tale). Needs a genuinely authentic Malaysian shot.
Recommendation: Variant A (brand-mark) for the splash. A boot screen is on the eye for a blink (the live gate is 300ms), so the job is "show the brand and get out of the way" - exactly what A does, instantly and with no decode cost. The photography direction explicitly allows the clean brand mark as the ONE exception for the boot frame (audit §6, decision 5: "Splash may be the one exception where a clean brand mark is fine"), and the emotional photography payoff is already delivered where it belongs - on the OnBoarding carousel (A4.1), which uses full-bleed photo + brand-tint and is the screen a new tutor actually dwells on. Variant B is here for the side-by-side; if the founder prefers it, ship it with a genuinely authentic Malaysian shot (not stock) and a static poster/colour behind it so a slow decode never shows blank.
Locked lessons obeyed. Clean · no duplicate words (the mark shows "Sifututor" once; no second wordmark) · no em-dashes in copy · "Sifututor" (one capital S) · neutral tagline ("Empowering Learning & Earning" - plain, no hype, no invented numbers) · new tokens only (--brand:#2B2FBE, the synced canonical - NO #2B2FBE, no raw hex in the app layer, no Theme.*).
Commercial pass (Rule 16). Founder: the splash is the cheapest, highest-frequency brand impression (every single launch); it sets the "is this legit" first read before the funnel even starts. Sales: no upsell belongs on a 300ms boot frame (transparency guard: nothing to decline here). Marketing: a consistent, on-brand boot reinforces recognition every open; variant B leans the human-service story harder. Analyst: instrument cold-start time-to-interactive (the boot frame must not add latency). Guard metric: app cold-start time must not regress - the chief reason A (no photo decode) is the safer boot choice.
