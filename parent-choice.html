Parent curated-choice · pick in-app, not in WhatsApp
After a request, the parent picks from a curated shortlist of verified tutors, confirms, and reviews each class. North Star: replace the manual WhatsApp hand-pick with a prompt in-app choice, which lifts matches and applications. For Hafiz · 24 June 2026.
The model is curated parent-choice: the admin verifies every tutor, the platform shortlists 3-5 that fit, the parent picks, and after each class leaves a neutral review. There is no trial class. The de-risk is the verification gate plus the review: a low review quietly flags a dissatisfied parent for the team to follow up and re-match. Today the parent app shows only a tutor count and "Profiles on the way!", with a half-built applicant card that was never wired in. This wave finishes that job.

Why no trial. Sifututor does not promote a trial class. The quality gate is the verification, every shortlisted tutor is already checked, so the parent picks a vetted tutor and the confirm simply sets expectations.

If a tutor is not the right fit, the parent does not hit a "request a re-match" button. They just leave their normal post-class review (Screen 3), and a low review is what flags them for the team to follow up and re-match. The de-risk is passive and costs the parent zero extra taps.

Tap-count: shortlist, open a showcase, Choose, Confirm = about 3 taps, versus scrolling a WhatsApp thread.

Notes for Hafiz
The neat property: one neutral review does double duty, the dissatisfaction signal (the de-risk) AND the showcase social proof. No trial, no negative prompt, no extra taps for an unhappy parent.
Backend dependencies: (1) a shortlist endpoint returning the curated `shortlist_tutors[]` in the request detail; (2) a choose/select-tutor endpoint + a SHORTLISTED state; (3) a reviews/ratings table + a post-class review prompt, with the low-score flag routed to the team; (4) a re-match action on the team side.
Open questions: the review threshold that flags a parent for follow-up; whether reviews show publicly on the showcase or stay internal at first; the team's re-match turnaround.
Scope: this is the parent's choose-and-review surface. It depends on the tutor showcase (its own wave, already designed) for the card, and it introduces the review that the tutor profile wanted. The pieces now meet.
