Legal / Policy reader · PolicyScreen (shared template)

In the live app, PolicyScreen is a generic viewer and PrivacyPolicy and TermAndConditions are thin wrappers that pass a policyType (privacy_statement / terms_of_service) into it. This mock redesigns the reader chrome only (header, last-updated line, scannable section structure, readable typography, dark mode). The policy body is live HTML served from /api/tutor/policies and is admin-managed in the backend database. It is not in the codebase and is not invented here. Section headings and greeked placeholder lines are representative structure only; see the annotated banner inside each frame.

Element (live)VerdictProvenance + reasoning
Nav bar header title (e.g. "Privacy Policy") KEEP Passed in from the wrapper (PrivacyPolicy / TermAndConditions) via policyType. Title is already present in the nav; promoted to both the nav-bar and the hero card title for visual hierarchy. No live data dropped.
Policy content HTML (the body, served from /api/tutor/policies) KEEP Admin-managed in the backend DB; the single source of truth. Not touched by this redesign. The reader chrome changes around it. Section headings in the frames are representative structure only, marked clearly as illustrative.
Per-policy white card (single View wrapping the HTML dump) KEEP Kept as the .policy-body card. Typography is improved (larger heading weight, generous line-height, hairline section dividers) but the server-HTML boundary is preserved.
Dual-policy support via policyType KEEP One template, two policyType values (privacy_statement / terms_of_service). Frames 1 and 2 confirm the same chrome serves both, with the title and section headings differing only by content.
"Last updated" date line ADD Currently absent from the reader chrome. Added as a muted meta line in DD-MMM-YYYY format (normalized to the single date format: 01-Jan-2025). Source: the policy record's updated_at field. Builds trust and compliance confidence.
Jump nav (in-page section list) ADD Policies are long documents. A numbered jump-to list above the body lets the tutor navigate directly to the section they need without scrolling the full dump. Section titles are rendered from the server HTML's heading tags (not hardcoded); shown here as representative headings.
"Current version" badge ADD A green trust chip next to the date. Signals the tutor is reading the live, published version (not a cached or stale copy). Small but meaningful for compliance.
Help centre footer link ADD A natural next step at the bottom of a legal document. Currently absent; a tutor who reads the policy and has questions has nowhere to go. One tappable link reduces legal-query support tickets.
Dark mode ADD The live screen has no dark-mode handling. Frame 3 shows the full reader in dark mode via the shared token system (no hardcoded hex).
1
One template, two policies. PolicyScreen is the shared reader; PrivacyPolicy and TermAndConditions are thin wrappers that pass policyType in. This mock redesigns the chrome once and both screens benefit. Frames 1 and 2 prove the same layout title, jump-nav, and body card serve both privacy_statement and terms_of_service.
2
Body stays server-driven, chrome changes around it. The policy copy is admin-managed in the backend DB and fetched at runtime from /api/tutor/policies. This redesign does NOT alter, invent, or present any real legal text. Section headings and greeked placeholder lines in all frames are illustrative structure only, clearly annotated with the dashed-border server banner.
3
Readability improvements. The before frame shows the live pain: a single undifferentiated white card of dense small-text with no section breaks, no last-updated line, no navigation, and no heading hierarchy. The after adds: (a) clear heading weight inside the body, (b) hairline dividers between sections, (c) a numbered jump-nav above the body for long-form navigation, (d) a "last updated" meta line, and (e) a help-centre footer. Typography scale uses CSS variables only (no raw px/hex).
4
Single date format normalized. The spec requires DD-MMM-YYYY across all screens (the live app mixes 24h News and 12h Notifications formats). The last-updated date on this reader uses 01-Jan-2025 format consistently. If the backend returns an ISO timestamp, the client must format it to this pattern before display.
Commercial pass (trust and compliance). Founder: a readable, dated, navigable policy is a trust signal, not a liability dump; tutors who understand what they are agreeing to onboard with more confidence and fewer churn-inducing surprises. Sales: no direct attach here, but a tutor who can quickly find the commission / termination section without scrolling has fewer drop-off moments during onboarding (the policy is often a step in the sign-up flow). Marketing: a polished legal reader communicates that Sifututor is a legitimate, professional platform, not a scrappy app with copy-pasted text; visible compliance is a brand asset, especially for parent-facing credibility. Analyst: guard metric: policy-scroll-depth (tutors should be able to get to "Contact us" in 2 taps via jump nav; measure whether support tickets citing policy confusion drop after the jump nav ships). All improvements are transparent and honest; no dark patterns present.
