Support · FAQs (search-first rework)

Rework v2 (founder direction 2026-07-01): v1 was a text-heavy wall, all 6 categories stacked with the first auto-expanded, so the tutor landed on a scroll of questions. This rework leads with ONE focus and uses progressive disclosure, grounded in how Apple Support, Intercom and Stripe help screens work: a search field as the hero (the static "What do you want to know?" becomes a real action), then Browse by topic = the 6 categories as collapsed rows that expand their questions inline. Nothing is cut: all 6 full category names and every question are preserved, just restructured and demoted. Search filters the already-loaded FAQ list client-side (no backend). Q&A copy shown here is illustrative; the real content is admin-managed server data. Tokens only, no em-dashes, both modes.
Brand = search, toggle icons, topic tiles Grouped-grey canvas White card surface BEFORE = live wall (heavy, all-stacked)

1
The fix for "text-heavy". You no longer land on a scroll of questions. The screen leads with one focus (search), then a calm 6-row browse list. The full content is all still here, one level down. This is restructure and demote, not cut.
2
Progressive disclosure (frame 2). Tapping a topic expands its questions inline (chevron rotates, questions slide in), and tapping a question opens its answer. One topic open at a time keeps the screen focused. A tutor sees only what they chose to open.
3
Tap counts. Searched question: type then tap the result (the fast path). Browsed question: 2 taps (open topic, open question). The wall got shorter and nothing got buried more than one extra level.

Commercial pass (Support deflection surface). Founder: a tutor who self-serves an answer is a tutor who keeps working instead of waiting on support; search raises the self-serve rate, which lowers support cost per active tutor. Sales: no direct upsell, but lower friction protects retention (every unanswered question is a churn risk for a gig worker). Marketing: a fast, well-organised help screen signals a professional platform during onboarding. Analyst: instrument search-use rate and the "Still need help" deflection rate (FAQ to support ticket); guard metric = the deflection footer must not bury a genuine support need (watch that ticket completion does not drop). Primary path: search to answer.
